Tasks to Complete Study

Sequence Exposure

Instructions

You will see a short (2-3 minute) sequence of images. Please pay attention to see if any pattern is present. Afterwards, you will be asked some questions about the sequence. A short break will be offered halfway through, so if you need to leave, please wait until then to do so.

Self-Paced Reading Frank

Instructions

You will be asked to read a number of sentences, using the spacebar to progress word by word. Some sentences will be followed by a yes/no question regarding it's contents.

WARNING: You must not hold down the spacebar to advance the text, but read each word and then press spacebar, word by word through the text. If you hold the button down to speed up or press without reading, it will invalidate your results and the data collected will be useless.

Raven

Instructions

Self-Paced Reading Short Story

Instructions

You will be asked to read a short story, again, using the spacebar to progress word by word. There will be no questions, but please do read carefully.

WARNING: You must not hold down the spacebar to advance the text, but read each word and then press spacebar, word by word through the text. If you hold the button down to speed up or press without reading, it will invalidate your results and the data collected will be useless.

Vocabulary Game

Instructions

You will be asked to take a short online vocabulary knowledge test - LexTALE - with instructions provided at the site. When you finish, leave the screen with the score displayed, and call the research assistant over to record your result. He will be unable to pay you without this score, and if you delete it you will have to take the entire test again.

Optional Essay Question

Instructions

Please open the 'notepad' app on the desktop computer, and save a .txt file to the Desktop with your subject ID. For example, edxU1234567F.txt would be the file name for a student with matric number U1234567F.

Once ready, notify your lab assistant for timing purposes, and begin a short essay on the prompt: "Is life easier for today's students as compared to the pioneer generation? Why?"
